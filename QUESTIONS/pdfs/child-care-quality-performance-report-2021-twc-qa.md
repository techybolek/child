# Questions and Answers: Texas Child Care Quality Performance Report FFY 2021

## Q1: How many licensed child care providers operated in Texas as of September 30, 2021?

**A:** As of September 30, 2021, Texas had:
- 9,383 licensed center-based programs
- 4,219 licensed family child care homes (registered and licensed homes)
- 16 license-exempt center-based programs
- 1,672 license-exempt family child care homes (Listed Family Homes)

---

## Q2: What is Texas Rising Star and how many providers participated in FFY 2021?

**A:** Texas Rising Star is the state's Quality Rating and Improvement System (QRIS). As of September 30, 2021, 2,043 providers (17.1% of subsidized providers) participated in Texas Rising Star, representing a 9% increase from 1,829 providers in FY2020. The program has three certification levels (Two-Star, Three-Star, and Four-Star) and provides professional development, resources, and mentoring to support high-quality child care.

---

## Q3: How many CCDF children were enrolled in Texas Rising Star programs?

**A:** The average number of CCDF children per day enrolled in Texas Rising Star programs increased from 42,202 to 53,499. As of September 30, 2021, 56,350 unduplicated subsidized children (25% of all subsidized children) were in Texas Rising Star-certified provider care.

---

## Q4: What professional development support did Texas provide to child care workers in 2021?

**A:** In 2021, Local Workforce Development Boards provided:
- Scholarships for more than 1,760 teachers
- Professional training on early learning guidelines to 211 programs
- Mentoring/technical assistance to over 2,898 programs

Additionally, TWC's Professional Development Scholarship initiative awarded 116 CDA scholarships, 110 Associate degree scholarships, 24 Bachelor degree scholarships, and supported 20 participants in apprenticeship programs.

---

## Q5: How many people were registered in the Texas Workforce Registry?

**A:** As of September 30, 2021, there were 57,146 total participants in the Texas Workforce Registry, including 53,274 practitioners and 3,872 directors. The registry is a web-based application for early childhood education professionals working with children birth to age eight to track their education, employment history, and training hours.

---

## Q6: What were Texas's main goals for child care quality improvement in FFY 2021?

**A:** Texas had three main quality improvement goals:
1. Improve the quality of child care services by assisting providers in achieving higher standards, including Texas Rising Star certification and national accreditation
2. Increase access to quality child care by increasing the number of Texas Rising Star certified and nationally accredited facilities
3. Improve child caregiver quality by increasing early childhood education professional development opportunities for child care and prekindergarten teachers

---

## Q7: How much did Texas spend on child care quality improvement in FFY 2021?

**A:** Texas spent $88,531,617 from CCDF quality set-aside funds on QRIS and quality improvement activities. This included statewide initiatives and local Workforce Board spending on various quality activities such as professional development, technical assistance, training, and support for Texas Rising Star certification.

---

## Q8: What health and safety inspection activities were conducted in Texas during FFY 2021?

**A:** Between October 1, 2020 and September 30, 2021, Texas conducted:
- 19,737 application and monitoring inspections
- 5,260 follow-up inspections
- 4,626 investigation inspections

The state received 10,610 complaints regarding licensed providers, with an average response time of 1.97 days. Technical assistance was offered 79,400 times related to minimum standards compliance.

---

## Q9: Did Texas provide COVID-19 relief funding to child care providers?

**A:** Yes, Texas used CRRSA funding to provide Child Care Relief Funds to open and operating regulated child care providers. As of September 30, 2021:
- Over 9,500 providers had applied
- 9,139 were approved
- $559,052,656 had been awarded

The average one-time award was $84,210 for licensed child care centers, $6,906 for licensed child care homes, and $6,499 for registered child care homes. Additionally, TWC provided 25% additional payments to providers serving subsidized children from April 2020 through March 2021.

---

## Q10: How many infant and toddler specialists were available to providers in Texas?

**A:** TWC funded an Infant and Toddler Specialist Network (ITSN) that began in January 2020 and continued through FY2021. The network included 56 specialists available to all providers and 56 specialists specifically available to providers serving children receiving CCDF. During the reporting period, these specialists served 193 providers and reached a total of 2,963 children. While on-site coaching was postponed due to COVID-19, specialists continued coaching teachers remotely.
