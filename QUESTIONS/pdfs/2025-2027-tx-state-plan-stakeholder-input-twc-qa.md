# 2025-2027 CCDF State Plan Stakeholder Input - Q&A

## Overview and Purpose

**Q1: What is the CCDF State Plan and what time period does it cover?**

A1: The CCDF (Child Care and Development Fund) State Plan is a comprehensive document that informs the Office of Child Care about Texas's child care policies, strategies, and vision for the next three years, covering 2025-2027. It describes the policies, strategies, and vision for the child care program, including both the subsidy program and Texas Rising Star quality rating system.

**Q2: What are the 10 main sections covered in the CCDF State Plan?**

A2: The CCDF State Plan includes ten comprehensive sections: (1) Program Administration, (2) Child and Family Eligibility and Enrollment and Continuity of Care, (3) Child Care Affordability, (4) Parental Choice, Equal Access, Payment Rates, and Payment Practices, (5) Health and Safety of Child Care Settings, (6) Support for a Skilled, Qualified, and Compensated Child Care Workforce, (7) Quality Improvement Activities, (8) Lead Agency Coordination and Partnerships to Support Service Delivery, (9) Family Outreach and Consumer Education, and (10) Program Integrity and Accountability.

**Q3: How many total survey responses were collected for the stakeholder input process, and what was the data collection period?**

A3: A total of 5,481 surveys were collected during the stakeholder input process, with data collected from January 15 through February 2, 2024. The data presented in the preliminary results was updated as of February 13, 2024.

## Survey Respondents

**Q4: What types of stakeholders participated in the survey, and which group had the highest response rate?**

A4: Five main stakeholder groups participated: Child Care Providers (2,750 responses, 50.17%), Parents (2,193 responses, 40.01%), Early Childhood Care & Education Stakeholders (288 responses, 5.25%), Local Workforce Development Board/Workforce Solutions staff or contractors (115 responses, 2.10%), and Employers not involved in child care businesses (135 responses, 2.46%). Child care providers had the highest participation rate.

**Q5: What types of child care operations were represented in the survey responses?**

A5: The survey included diverse child care operation types: Licensed Child Care Centers represented the largest group at 75.99%, followed by Licensed Child Care Homes at 13.06%, Registered Child Care Homes at 9.63%, and Other operation types at 1.32%.

**Q6: What percentage of survey respondents provide care for children receiving subsidies?**

A6: Among child care providers surveyed, 73.05% currently provide care for children receiving subsidies, 8.02% accept subsidies but have no children currently enrolled, and 18.93% do not accept subsidies.

## Priority Topics and Issues

**Q7: What were the top three most important topics identified by all respondents?**

A7: The top three priority topics across all respondents were: (1) Texas Rising Star at 44.28%, making it the highest priority by a significant margin, (2) Pre-K Partnerships at 31.50%, and (3) Child Care for Children with Disabilities or Special Needs at 23.58%. These topics clearly emerged as the most critical areas of concern for the Texas child care community.

**Q8: How do priority topics differ between child care providers and parents?**

A8: While both groups prioritized Texas Rising Star and Pre-K Partnerships as their top two concerns, there were notable differences in their third priorities. Child care providers ranked Child Care for Children with Disabilities or Special Needs third, followed by Statewide Family Child Care Network and Capacity Expansion. Parents, however, prioritized Statewide Family Child Care Network third, followed by Child Care for Children with Disabilities or Special Needs and Employer-Supported Child Care, reflecting their focus on access and availability.

**Q9: What is the Statewide Family Child Care Network initiative, and why is it important to stakeholders?**

A9: The Statewide Family Child Care Network is a staffed program designed to support a network of family child care providers through a comprehensive menu of services including training and professional development, networking opportunities, technical assistance, and connections to local and state resources. It ranked fourth overall in importance with 25.78% support, indicating strong recognition of the need to strengthen and support family-based child care operations across Texas.

**Q10: What is the Business Coaching initiative and how does it support the child care sector?**

A10: Business Coaching addresses the unique needs of child care business owners through specialized coaching designed to improve business practices and build stronger, more stable child care businesses. This initiative received 25.54% support among respondents, ranking seventh overall, with child care providers showing particularly strong interest by ranking it sixth in their priorities, recognizing the critical need for business management skills in running successful child care operations.

## Specific Initiatives

**Q11: What goals were identified for Texas Rising Star based on stakeholder input?**

A11: Stakeholders identified the need to establish goals for increasing the number of subsidy providers participating in Texas Rising Star and developing a structured outreach campaign to educate both parents and providers about the program. This was identified as the top priority issue across all stakeholder groups.

**Q12: What is the purpose of Pre-K Partnerships and why did it rank so highly?**

A12: Pre-K Partnerships involve collaborative efforts with the Texas Education Agency to help build and expand the availability of prekindergarten services within child care programs. It ranked second overall at 31.50% importance, reflecting strong stakeholder interest in integrating early education opportunities into existing child care settings to better serve families and prepare children for school.

**Q13: What initiatives are included under Work-Based Learning Staffing Initiatives?**

A13: Work-Based Learning Staffing Initiatives include multiple approaches to addressing workforce challenges: supporting high school or college students gaining experience in child care classrooms for internships or practicums, providing scholarships and supports to child care teachers currently in school, and supporting Registered Apprenticeship Programs. This comprehensive approach to workforce development received 33.61% support from one stakeholder group.

**Q14: How does the Capacity Expansion initiative aim to address child care availability?**

A14: The Capacity Expansion initiative focuses on increasing the supply of high-quality child care through two main strategies: promoting the creation of new child care businesses and increasing the number of available slots in existing programs, with particular attention to addressing needs in child care deserts. This initiative received 25.48% support and ranked fifth overall in importance.

**Q15: What is Employer-Supported Child Care and what assistance does it provide?**

A15: Employer-Supported Child Care provides technical assistance to employers interested in exploring the development of employer child care programs either on-site or nearby their workplaces. This initiative received 28.68% support from one stakeholder group and was ranked fifth by parents, indicating recognition that employer involvement can help address child care access challenges for working families.

## Inclusion and Special Needs

**Q16: What efforts are being made to support children with disabilities or special needs in child care settings?**

A16: The state is focusing on strengthening inclusion practices in early childhood environments, with this initiative ranking third overall at 23.58% importance. All stakeholder groups recognized this as a critical priority, with ECE stakeholders ranking it second in their priorities, demonstrating broad consensus on the need to improve access and quality of care for children with disabilities or special needs.

**Q17: What is Family Engagement and why is it important to the state plan?**

A17: Family Engagement focuses on creating more opportunities for family feedback to improve the quality and accessibility of early learning services. While it ranked ninth overall at 33.11% from one group, parents ranked it seventh in their priorities, indicating their desire to have meaningful input into the child care system and services that affect their families.

## Process and Timeline

**Q18: What was the complete timeline for developing and submitting the 2025-2027 CCDF State Plan?**

A18: The comprehensive timeline includes: Stakeholder Input Survey (January 15-February 2, 2024), Texas Early Learning Council meeting (January 19, 2024), Stakeholder Input meetings (February 8-28, 2024), Commission Action on Draft State Plan (March 26, 2024), Public Hearing for Comment on Draft State Plan (April 2024), Final State Plan Adopted (June 18, 2024), and Final State Plan Submitted to Office of Child Care (June 30, 2024).

**Q19: Where and when were stakeholder input meetings held for the CCDF State Plan?**

A19: Six stakeholder input meetings were scheduled across Texas: Abilene (Thursday, February 8, 2024), Round Rock (Tuesday, February 13, 2024), Houston (Wednesday, February 21, 2024), McAllen (Thursday, February 22, 2024), Dallas (Monday, February 26, 2024), and a Statewide Virtual meeting (Wednesday, February 28, 2024). These locations were strategically selected to ensure geographic representation across the state.

A20: Stakeholders can contact Cathi Arwood from the Child Care & Early Learning Division at the Texas Workforce Commission via email at ccel@twc.texas.gov for stakeholder input. Additional information is available on the Texas Workforce Commission website at https://www.twc.texas.gov/programs/child-care.

## Organizational Structure

**Q21: How is the child care system organized in Texas and what agencies are involved?**

A21: The Texas child care system operates through a multi-level structure with the US Department of Health & Human Services, Administration of Children & Families, and Office of Child Care at the federal level. At the state level, the Texas Workforce Commission serves as the lead agency, working with the Texas Health and Human Services Commission (which handles child care regulation) and partnering with Local Workforce Development Boards, Child Care Services Contractors, and other partners to deliver Texas Rising Star and child care subsidy programs.

**Q22: What role do Local Workforce Development Boards play in the child care system?**

A22: Local Workforce Development Boards serve as key intermediaries in the child care system, working between the Texas Workforce Commission and Child Care Services Contractors to implement programs at the local level. They are responsible for administering child care subsidies and Texas Rising Star programs in their respective regions, making them critical partners in service delivery across the state.
