# Texas Rising Star Parent Brochure (Vietnamese) - Q&A Pairs

Q1: What is Texas Rising Star and what makes it different from regular child care programs?
A1: Texas Rising Star is a quality rating system for early childhood education programs in Texas. Programs with Texas Rising Star certification focus on quality and meet higher standards than the state's minimum child care licensing requirements. These programs are committed to providing higher quality child care that helps children succeed.

Q2: What are the certification levels in the Texas Rising Star program and what do they mean?
A2: Texas Rising Star has three certification levels: Two Stars (meets some quality standards), Three Stars (meets most quality standards), and Four Stars (meets nearly all quality standards). The certification is shown as filled stars out of four total stars, with more filled stars indicating higher quality standards met.

Q3: What specific benefits do Texas Rising Star certified programs provide that regular programs may not?
A3: Texas Rising Star programs offer several key benefits including smaller class sizes with more teachers, teachers with higher education levels and regular training, teachers who think positively and are patient, age-appropriate activities and quality curriculum, indoor and outdoor classrooms that help children learn and develop, support for learning at home, and community resources for families.

Q4: How can parents find a Texas Rising Star certified child care provider in their area?
A4: Parents can visit https://find.childcare.texas.gov to find child care services in their area. Texas Rising Star programs are highlighted on this website. Parents should remember to click on "View certificates and inspection reports" in the child care program profile to see if there have been any health or safety violations.

Q5: What should parents consider when determining their child care budget?
A5: Parents should first determine how much they can spend on child care each month. They should then visit their local Workforce Solutions office to find out if their family qualifies for child care subsidies, which can help make quality child care more affordable.

Q6: What types of child care settings can receive Texas Rising Star certification?
A6: Texas Rising Star certification can be awarded to various types of child care settings including child care centers, after-school programs, registered or licensed family homes, and other care settings outside of regular work hours.

Q7: What are the four steps parents should follow to choose high-quality child care?
A7: The four steps are: (1) Find child care using the online search tool and check for Texas Rising Star programs and inspection reports, (2) Determine your budget and check if you qualify for child care subsidies, (3) Understand the different types of child care available (centers, after-school programs, family homes), and (4) Be a smart consumer by visiting programs that meet your basic needs and requesting a tour.

Q8: What important developmental fact about children is highlighted in the brochure?
A8: The brochure emphasizes that 90% of brain development occurs before age 5. This underscores the critical importance of choosing high-quality early childhood programs that support children's development and prepare them for future success.

Q9: What can parents do if their child's current program is not Texas Rising Star certified?
A9: Parents can help their current child care provider participate in the certification process. They should ask their provider to visit www.TexasRisingStar.org for more details about how to become certified. The program offers support to help child care providers achieve certification.

Q10: Where can parents get more information about Texas Rising Star and contact their local workforce development board?
A10: Parents can visit www.TexasRisingStar.org for more information about how to choose a high-quality program for their child and to contact their local workforce development board.
