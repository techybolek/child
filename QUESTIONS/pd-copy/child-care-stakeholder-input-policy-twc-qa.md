# Child Care Stakeholder Input Policy - Question & Answer Pairs

## Q1: What legislative action prompted TWC to develop a stakeholder input policy for the Child Care program?
**A1:** Senate Bill 208, enacted by the 84th Texas Legislature in 2015, added section 302.00435 to the Texas Labor Code, requiring TWC's three-member Commission to develop a policy for obtaining input from interested parties regarding its subsidized child care program. This legislation was prompted by a January 2015 Sunset Advisory Commission Staff Report recommendation and required TWC to adopt the policy no later than September 1, 2016.

## Q2: Which state agencies are identified as partners in TWC's child care stakeholder input process?
**A2:** The state agency partners identified in the Child Care and Development Fund State Plan include the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC), Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS), Texas Education Agency (TEA), Head Start State Collaboration Office (HSSCO), and the Texas Early Childhood Professional Development System (TECPDS). These agencies conduct regular meetings to coordinate on child care and early education matters.

## Q3: How does TWC gather input from Local Workforce Development Boards regarding child care policies?
**A3:** TWC staff participates in Child Care network meetings that comprise members from each of the 28 Local Workforce Development Boards. These network meetings review child care policies, procedures, quality initiatives, and other child care-related matters to gather input on the Child Care program, and staff reports this feedback to the Commission.

## Q4: What additional requirements did House Bill 680 from the 86th Legislature add to TWC's stakeholder input policy?
**A4:** House Bill 680, passed in 2019, amended section 302.00435 of the Texas Labor Code to require TWC's policy to include methods for obtaining input from the Texas Education Agency, school districts, open-enrollment charter schools, subsidized child care providers, relevant businesses, and the public. The bill specifically focuses on improving coordination between subsidized child care and prekindergarten programs, increasing quality and access, improving health and safety regulations, and identifying regulatory burdens that could be reduced.

## Q5: What topics can stakeholders provide input on during TWC's public meetings and workgroups?
**A5:** TWC encourages stakeholders and the public to provide input and comments on all aspects of the state's subsidized child care system, including delivery of subsidized child care services, alignment of early education programs, quality initiatives and standards (including Texas Rising Star), and other topics central to early childhood education.

## Q6: How often does the Commission conduct public meetings to solicit input on the Child Care program?
**A6:** The Commission conducts public meetings each biennium to solicit input on the Child Care program from child care stakeholders and the general public. According to the document, four such meetings are scheduled per biennium to ensure adequate opportunities for stakeholder engagement.

## Q7: What are the digital and modern methods TWC uses to gather stakeholder input?
**A7:** TWC utilizes several digital methods to gather stakeholder input, including social media posts, email communications, and comments submitted via the TWC website. Staff also recommended developing a dedicated web page on TWC's website to inform the public of TWC's policy on stakeholder input and opportunities to provide input.

## Q8: What specific areas related to health and safety regulations does TWC seek provider input on?
**A8:** TWC seeks input from child care providers on two key areas: first, existing health and safety regulations that could be more efficient or less costly without reducing health and safety outcomes; and second, burdens related to complying with existing regulations that could be mitigated, reduced, or eliminated while maintaining the intent, objective, or purpose of the underlying regulation.

## Q9: What specialized forums and councils does TWC use to gather stakeholder input?
**A9:** TWC gathers input from several specialized forums including the Texas Early Learning Council (of which TWC, TEA, and the Texas Health and Human Services Commission are members), specialized interagency and stakeholder workgroups such as the Texas Rising Star Four-Year Review Workgroup, and focus groups held at the annual Texas Early Childhood Learning Summit.

## Q10: How does TWC ensure coordination between subsidized child care programs and prekindergarten programs through stakeholder input?
**A10:** TWC provides specific opportunities for the Texas Education Agency, school districts, open-enrollment charter schools, relevant businesses, and the public to offer input on coordination between subsidized child care and pre-K programs. This includes participation in the Texas Early Learning Council, quarterly meetings with agency partners, regional meetings with local stakeholders, and various digital communication channels to ensure comprehensive feedback on improving program alignment.
